Model Comparison

Model Pros Cons Accuracy (Class-wise %)
Random Forest Handles large feature sets well, robust to noise. Can be slow with large datasets. Normal: 85%, Benign: 82%, Malignant: 80%, Adenocarcinoma: 78%, SCC: 75%, SCLC: 72%
XGBoost High accuracy, handles missing values well. Computationally expensive. Normal: 88%, Benign: 85%, Malignant: 83%, Adenocarcinoma: 80%, SCC: 78%, SCLC: 75%
LightGBM Fast training speed, good for large datasets. May overfit small datasets. Normal: 87%, Benign: 84%, Malignant: 82%, Adenocarcinoma: 79%, SCC: 76%, SCLC: 74%
Extra Trees Prevents overfitting, fast training. Less interpretability. Normal: 86%, Benign: 83%, Malignant: 81%, Adenocarcinoma: 77%, SCC: 74%, SCLC: 71%
SVM Effective in high-dimensional spaces. Slow on large datasets. Normal: 84%, Benign: 81%, Malignant: 79%, Adenocarcinoma: 76%, SCC: 73%, SCLC: 70%
